# City Services Documentation

This document contains source information for the CivicNav knowledge base.

## Waste Management

### Trash Collection

- **Residential Schedule**: Monday and Thursday (most areas), Tuesday and Friday (downtown)
- **Collection Time**: Place bins at curb by 7:00 AM
- **Requirements**: Bins 3 feet apart, away from obstacles
- **Large Items**: First Monday of each month (by appointment)

### Recycling

- **Schedule**: Every Wednesday citywide
- **Accepted Materials**: Paper, cardboard, glass, aluminum, plastics #1-7
- **Not Accepted**: Plastic bags, styrofoam, food-contaminated items
- **Drop-off Center**: 500 Green Way, Saturdays 8 AM - 4 PM

## Permits and Licenses

### Building Permits

- **Form**: BP-100
- **Location**: City Hall, Room 201 or online
- **Requirements**: Property survey, architectural plans, contractor license, proof of ownership
- **Processing Time**: 2-4 weeks standard, expedited available
- **Inspections**: Foundation, framing, final

### Business Licenses

- **Requirements**: Application form, state tax ID, zoning verification, professional licenses
- **Fees**: $50-$500 annually based on type and revenue
- **Renewal**: Due January 31 each year
- **Home Businesses**: Require home occupation permit

## Emergency Services

### Emergency Numbers

- **911**: Life-threatening emergencies
- **555-0100**: Police non-emergency
- **555-0200**: Fire non-emergency
- **Emergency Management**: 24/7 operations center

### Snow Emergencies

- **Parking Prohibition**: Designated snow emergency routes
- **Move Deadline**: Within 4 hours of declaration
- **Penalty**: $100 ticket plus towing
- **Announcements**: Emergency alerts, social media, local news

## Public Works

### 311 Services

- **Hours**: M-F 7 AM - 7 PM, Sat 9 AM - 5 PM
- **Services**: Potholes, streetlights, graffiti, abandoned vehicles, noise complaints
- **Response Times**: Potholes 72 hours, streetlights 5 days, graffiti 48 hours

### Sidewalk Repairs

- **Responsibility**: Property owners (adjacent sidewalks)
- **Cost-Share Program**: City covers 50% for qualifying homeowners
- **Priority**: Trip hazards over 1 inch, high pedestrian areas
- **Emergency**: 48-hour response for dangerous conditions

### Tree Services

- **Trimming Cycle**: 5 years
- **Permits**: Required for removal of trees over 6" diameter
- **Free Mulch**: Available at Public Works yard
- **Plant a Tree Program**: Free front yard trees (spring applications)

## Community Services

### Library System

- **Main Library**: Downtown
- **Branch Libraries**: 8 locations
- **Library Cards**: Free for residents
- **Services**: Books, digital resources, computers, meeting rooms, makerspace
- **Overdue Fines**: Eliminated

### Senior Services

- **Senior Center**: 200 Oak Street
- **Hours**: M-F 9 AM - 4 PM (age 60+)
- **Meals**: Lunch at noon ($3 donation), Meals on Wheels available
- **Transit**: Senior passes $20/month unlimited
- **Contact**: 555-SENIOR

### Parks and Recreation

- **Community Centers**: 12 locations citywide
- **Programs**: Youth, adult, senior activities
- **Registration**: Quarterly, online or in person
- **Financial Assistance**: Available for qualifying families
- **Dog Parks**: 4 off-leash parks (dawn to dusk)

## Transportation

### Street Parking

- **Downtown Meters**: M-Sat 8 AM - 6 PM
- **Payment**: Coins, credit cards, ParkMobile app
- **Residential Permits**: $35 annually
- **Violations**: $35-$100

### Voter Information

- **Registration Deadline**: 21 days before election
- **Requirements**: U.S. citizen, 18+ by election day, 30-day residency
- **Early Voting**: 14 days before election at City Hall
- **Absentee Ballots**: Available by request

## Events and Programs

### Farmers Market

- **Summer**: Saturdays May-October, 8 AM - 1 PM, Central Square
- **Winter**: First and third Saturdays November-April, 9 AM - noon, Community Center
- **Vendors**: 50+ local vendors
- **SNAP/EBT**: Accepted with dollar-for-dollar match (up to $20)

### City Council

- **Meetings**: First and third Tuesday, 7 PM, City Hall
- **Public Comment**: Sign up in person or submit written by noon
- **Broadcast**: Cable channel and YouTube live
- **Archives**: Meeting minutes and recordings online

### Youth Employment

- **Summer Program**: Ages 14-21
- **Applications**: March 1 - April 15
- **Duration**: 8 weeks starting mid-June
- **Hours**: 20-25 per week at minimum wage
